Kings

Amelia Fink (2025)

I have a friend I call Bluejay.

Bluejay is a boy with feathers in his hair, and two blue strands braided on either side of his head. His brown roots peek out of his bleach-blonde hair. He has a pair of denim-coloured shoes that are scuffed at the toe and splattered with mud. He wears flannel shirts and jeans with holes. His hands are stained with paint. The backpack he carries is plastered with keychains and pins; they make noise when he walks. You can sometimes hear him coming.

I don’t remember meeting Bluejay.

Bluejay doesn’t like introducing himself to strangers, so I do it for him. I tell people, this is Bluejay, and sometimes they look slightly disgusted, sneering as I shake their hands. Other times, their smiles drop, before fumbling to pick them up. Sometimes they don’t try at all. Sometimes I hear them say things to Mum afterward.

- - -

People at school don’t like to sit with Bluejay and I. But they like to stare when they walk past. I ask Bluejay why they do it. He just says that if everyone in the world had a Bluejay, then no one would be staring at us.

I tell him, I don’t want to share him, and it makes him smile. Bluejay’s smile is crooked. He has colourful braces. I love when Bluejay smiles because I feel my heart sing and my skin buzz.

- - -

Bluejay is there when I have stupid ideas.

Like when he helps me bleach my hair when Mum leaves for work. He washes it out as I lean over the bathtub. Being upside down and halfway into the bath makes me giggle. When he finishes bleaching my hair, Bluejay dyes and braids two strands on either side of my head, blue like his, and then does my makeup. He does my eyeliner like his, but takes a blue from the makeup bag we stole from my cousin who lives with us. He makes stars around my eyes. What do you think, he asks. I didn’t know he could do makeup this good, I say. He asks what I think again, and I say, the blue matches our hair.

And then Mum walks into the bathroom and screams.

- - -

My cousin gets a boyfriend and stops spending time with me. I ask her why having a boyfriend means she can’t hang out with me anymore. Because of Bluejay, she says, I don’t want him to start asking questions. I don’t care if he asks questions, I tell her. She says, he just won’t get it.

I cry myself to sleep. She barely liked you anyway, Bluejay says.

- - -

When we’re alone, Bluejay mutters things under his breath. He’ll say, Mum is afraid of you. Everyone is afraid of you. It’s better that they stay away. You’re broken and you’ll break them too. I sometimes wonder if he thinks I can’t hear him. Or if he wants me to.

I always want Bluejay by my side, but he ditches me when I need him most. Standing up in front of the class, I’ll look for him. And he won’t be there. I stumble, words too big for my mouth and my limbs all mismatched. Later, when I ask him about it, he always says the same thing: I didn’t mean to. I’m sorry. I’ll accept the apology, but think about the things he mutters to himself.

- - -

Bluejay shows me how to dress like him. His clothes fit me like they were always mine. Bluejay is talking. I’m not really listening. I catch the end of him saying something about my cousin, and how we’re better off alone.

I ask Bluejay why no one’s ever dated me. Because you don’t like romance, he says. Right, I say. Or maybe it’s just scared of me. Bluejay moves strands of my hair around at random.

He says, Maybe you’re the scared one.

- - -

I have a friend I call Bluejay.

Bluejay tucks my hair behind my ear when it’s windy. Bluejay wipes the tears from my eyes when I cry. Bluejay holds my hand when I get my ears pierced. Bluejay stands in line with me for hours; leaves me standing alone in front of crowds. Bluejay tells me to speak up when the hairdresser is about to cut too much; says nothing when Mum yells at me. Bluejay carries me to bed after a bad day, buys me chocolate after a good day.

Bluejay protects me, overprotects me, takes care of me, neglects me.

When I look in the mirror, I see Bluejay. Bleached hair, blue braids, feathers. Denim shoes and dirt, flannel shirt, ripped jeans. Paint-stained hands. Backpack, pins and keychains.

Bluejay is the best friend I’ll ever have.
