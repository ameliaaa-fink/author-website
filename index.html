Amelia (Meels) Fink

Writer | Student | Lvl. 20

Amelia Fink (aka Meels!!) is an aspiring writer currently purusuing her passion at RMIT Melbourne through a Bachelors in Creative Writing. She is currently writing a novel which has been in the works for around 3 or 4 years! (There has been many scrapped drafts and much procrastination (ᴗ˳ᴗ)ᶻ𝗓𐰁 .ᐟ)

She mainly writes fiction, gearing towards the speculative-fiction, dystopian, and magical realism genres. She loves to dabble with psychological horror and queer horror as subgenres, which often gives her work that intricate, metaphorical touch of eerie. Through character-driven narratives and immersive world-building, she explores themes of humanity, love, and the ugly, complex, beautiful human experience.

Her journey into Digital Writing began because of her studies, but has since grown. She's always had a passion for video games, and has always believed that they have a potential unlike any other medium of storytelling. She hopes to be continue to pursue digital writing and/or gamemaking, and to keep using them as a means to promote empathy and to tell stories for the weird, the unusual, the psychologically complex, and the outcasted. Her most recent (and first proper) work in the digital writing field is Twine game called Messages from Kyle, which is psychological horror story where you play as someone navigating an old, second-hand computer. As you sift through files messages and discover the previous owners’ life (Lou), you slowly find hints of a darker, more confusing entity which seems to have possessed or hacked the computer, who guys by the name Kyle, and claims to be Lou's best friend. It’s unclear whether this is a human, stalker, or something more supernatural. The computer gets more and more glitchy and Kyle claims to ‘help’ whenever an error pops up. The more you chat with him, the more you discover that his and Lou's confusing relationship may have ended catastrophically...

Besides writing, Meels is passionate about game design and storytelling, coding, animation, and her friends! xx
